01

What is FIRE?

FIRE stands for Financial Independence, Retire Early. It's a movement focused on extreme savings and investment that allows people to retire far earlier than traditional budgets and retirement plans would allow.

1

Save Aggressively

FIRE followers typically save 50-70% of their income, far above the traditional 10-15%.

2

Invest Wisely

Money is invested in low-cost index funds, real estate, and other assets that grow over time.

3

Reach Your Number

Your "FIRE number" is typically 25x your annual expenses, based on the 4% safe withdrawal rule.

4

Live on Your Terms

Once you reach your number, work becomes optional. Pursue passions, travel, or keep working—it's your choice.

02

FIRE Types Explained

Not all FIRE journeys look the same. Choose the path that fits your lifestyle and goals.

Lean FIRE

Achieve financial independence with minimal expenses. Target: 25x your lean annual expenses (£25k-£40k/year).

  • Live on £25,000-£40,000 per year
  • Requires frugal lifestyle mindset
  • Fastest path to FIRE (often 10-15 years)
  • Best for minimalists and low cost areas
Target Portfolio £625K - £1M

Barista FIRE

Semi-retirement with part-time work for benefits and extra income. Your investments cover most expenses.

  • Work part-time for health insurance
  • Investments cover 70-80% of expenses
  • More flexibility than traditional retirement
  • Great transition strategy before full FIRE
Target Portfolio £500K - £800K

Coast FIRE

You've saved enough that compound growth will fund retirement. Work only for current expenses.

  • Front-load savings early in career
  • Let compound interest do the work
  • Reduce stress by securing future retirement
  • Switch to passion projects or lower-paying jobs
Key Milestone £200K by age 30

Fat FIRE

Retire with a luxurious lifestyle. Target: £100k+ annual spending with a portfolio of £2.5M+.

  • Maintain or upgrade current lifestyle
  • Requires higher income or longer timeline
  • More buffer for unexpected expenses
  • Travel, hobbies, and experiences without limits
Target Portfolio £2.5M+
03

Investment Vehicles

Understanding where to put your money is crucial for building wealth efficiently.

Index Funds

Low-cost funds that track market indices like S&P 500 or FTSE 100. The backbone of most FIRE portfolios due to diversification and low fees.

Average Return 7-10% annually

ISAs & Pensions

Tax-advantaged accounts in the UK. Stocks & Shares ISA allows £20k/year tax-free. Pensions offer tax relief on contributions.

ISA Limit £20,000/year

Real Estate

Rental properties or REITs for passive income and diversification. Can accelerate your FIRE timeline with leveraged returns.

Target Returns 8-12% cash-on-cash

Bonds

Lower risk, steady income. Increase allocation as you approach FIRE for portfolio stability and reduced volatility.

Common Rule Age = Bond %
04

The 4% Rule Explained

The 4% Rule is the foundation of FIRE planning. It determines how much you need to save to retire.

FIRE Number = Annual Expenses x 25

Why does this work?

The Trinity Study found that withdrawing 4% of your portfolio annually gives you a 95% chance of your money lasting 30+ years. Since 4% x 25 = 100%, you need 25 times your annual expenses.

Example Calculation

Monthly Expenses £3,000
Annual Expenses (x12) £36,000
FIRE Number (x25) £900,000

With £900,000 invested, you can safely withdraw £36,000/year (4%) to cover your expenses.

05

Pro Tips for FIRE

1

Pay Yourself First

Automate savings to investment accounts on payday. Treat savings like a non-negotiable bill. What you don't see, you won't spend.

2

Track Your Net Worth

Monthly tracking keeps you motivated and helps identify spending leaks. Watching your number grow is incredibly motivating.

3

Optimize the Big 3

Housing, transportation, and food are 60-70% of most budgets. Small wins in these categories have massive long-term impact.

4

Increase Income

While cutting expenses has limits, income growth is unlimited. Negotiate raises, switch jobs, start side hustles, or build passive income.

5

Tax Efficiency

Use ISAs to shelter investments from tax. Maximize pension contributions for tax relief. Tax saved is money earned.

6

Stay the Course

Market dips are normal. Don't panic sell. Time in the market beats timing the market. Keep investing through the ups and downs.

06

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Lifestyle Inflation

As income grows, expenses often grow too. The key to FIRE is keeping lifestyle costs flat while income increases. Save your raises.

Timing the Market

Studies consistently show that time IN the market beats timing the market. Stick to your investment plan regardless of market conditions.

Ignoring Healthcare

Early retirees face healthcare costs before state pension age. Budget for private health insurance or out-of-pocket expenses.

No Emergency Fund

Keep 3-6 months of expenses in liquid savings. This prevents selling investments at a loss during market downturns or unexpected expenses.

Neglecting the Present

FIRE is a marathon, not a sprint. Don't sacrifice all enjoyment today. Find balance between saving for tomorrow and living today.

No Plan for Retirement

Know what you'll do when you FIRE. Many struggle with purpose after leaving work. Have hobbies, projects, or part-time work planned.
