Learn Getting Started

What is FIRE?

FIRE stands for Financial Independence, Retire Early. It's a lifestyle movement focused on extreme savings and smart investing to achieve financial freedom decades earlier than traditional retirement.

Key Insight

FIRE isn't about never working again. It's about having the freedom to choose how you spend your time, without financial constraints.

The Core Principles

1

Save Aggressively

FIRE followers typically save 50-70% of their income, far above the traditional 10-15%.

2

Invest Wisely

Money is invested in low-cost index funds, real estate, and other assets that grow over time.

3

Reach Your Number

Your "FIRE number" is typically 25x your annual expenses, based on the 4% rule.

4

Live on Your Terms

Once you reach your number, work becomes optional. Pursue passions, travel, or keep working.

Your FIRE Number

Your FIRE number is the amount of money you need invested to sustain your lifestyle indefinitely without working.

The Formula
FIRE Number = Annual Expenses × 25

Example Calculation

Monthly Expenses £3,000
Annual Expenses (×12) £36,000
FIRE Number (×25) £900,000

With £900,000 invested, you can safely withdraw £36,000/year (4%) to cover your expenses indefinitely.

The 4% Rule

The 4% rule is the foundation of FIRE planning. It determines how much you can safely withdraw from your portfolio each year.

How It Works

The Trinity Study found that withdrawing 4% of your portfolio annually gives you a 95% chance of your money lasting 30+ years. Since 4% × 25 = 100%, you need 25 times your annual expenses.

Historical Success

Based on historical data, a 4% withdrawal rate has survived major market crashes, including the Great Depression and 2008 financial crisis.

Important Considerations

  • The 4% rule assumes a 30-year retirement. For earlier retirement, consider 3.5% or 3%.
  • Your portfolio should be diversified across stocks and bonds.
  • Adjust withdrawals based on market conditions (flexible spending).
  • Consider other income sources like part-time work or rental income.
Learn FIRE Types

Lean FIRE

Achieve financial independence with minimal expenses. Live a frugal but fulfilling life.

Annual Spending £25,000 - £40,000
Target Portfolio £625K - £1M
Timeline 10-15 years

Who It's For

  • Minimalists who don't need luxury
  • People in low cost-of-living areas
  • Those who want to retire as quickly as possible
  • People comfortable with a tight budget
Consideration

Lean FIRE leaves little room for unexpected expenses. Build an emergency fund and consider healthcare costs carefully.

Barista FIRE

Semi-retirement with part-time work. Your investments cover most expenses while you work for benefits and extra income.

Portfolio Coverage 70-80% of expenses
Target Portfolio £500K - £800K
Work Required Part-time

Benefits

  • Access to employer health insurance benefits
  • Supplemental income reduces portfolio stress
  • Social interaction and purpose from work
  • Great transition strategy before full FIRE

Coast FIRE

You've saved enough that compound growth will fund your traditional retirement. Work only covers current expenses.

Key Milestone £200K by age 30
Strategy Front-load savings
Retirement Age Traditional (60-67)

How It Works

Save aggressively early in your career, then let compound interest do the heavy lifting. Once you hit your Coast FIRE number, you can:

  • Switch to a lower-paying job you love
  • Work fewer hours
  • Take more risks in your career
  • Focus on work-life balance

Fat FIRE

Retire with a luxurious lifestyle. No compromises, no budgeting stress, full financial freedom.

Annual Spending £100,000+
Target Portfolio £2.5M+
Timeline 15-25 years

What It Allows

  • Maintain or upgrade your current lifestyle
  • Travel extensively and in comfort
  • Large buffer for unexpected expenses
  • Support family members financially
  • Pursue expensive hobbies without limits
Learn Investment Basics

Index Funds

Low-cost funds that track market indices like S&P 500 or FTSE 100. The backbone of most FIRE portfolios.

Average Return 7-10% annually
Typical Fees 0.03% - 0.20%

Why Index Funds?

  • Low fees: Expense ratios as low as 0.03%
  • Diversification: Own hundreds of companies in one fund
  • Simplicity: No stock picking or market timing required
  • Proven track record: Outperforms most actively managed funds

Popular Choices

  • Vanguard FTSE Global All Cap Index Fund
  • Vanguard S&P 500 UCITS ETF
  • iShares Core MSCI World UCITS ETF

ISAs & Pensions

Tax-advantaged accounts are crucial for maximizing your wealth. Every pound saved in tax is a pound invested.

Stocks & Shares ISA

Annual Limit £20,000
Tax on Growth 0%
Withdrawal Anytime, tax-free

Pensions (SIPP)

  • Tax relief on contributions (20-45% depending on income)
  • Employer matching (free money!)
  • Accessible from age 55 (rising to 57)
  • 25% tax-free lump sum at retirement
FIRE Strategy

Max out ISA first for flexibility, then contribute to pension for tax benefits. Use ISA to bridge the gap until pension access age.

Real Estate

Property investment can accelerate your FIRE journey through rental income and leveraged appreciation.

Target Returns 8-12% cash-on-cash
Passive Option REITs

Options

  • Buy-to-let: Direct property ownership with rental income
  • REITs: Real Estate Investment Trusts - property exposure through stocks
  • Crowdfunding: Invest in property with smaller amounts

Bonds

Lower risk, steady income. Important for portfolio stability as you approach FIRE.

Common Rule Age = Bond %
Typical Return 3-5% annually

Role in FIRE

  • Reduce portfolio volatility
  • Provide stable income in retirement
  • Rebalancing buffer during market crashes
  • Sleep-at-night factor for risk-averse investors

Saving Tips

Practical strategies to maximize your savings rate and accelerate your path to FIRE.

1

Pay Yourself First

Automate savings to investment accounts on payday. Treat savings like a non-negotiable bill.

2

Track Your Net Worth

Monthly tracking keeps you motivated and helps identify spending leaks.

3

Optimize the Big 3

Housing, transportation, and food are 60-70% of most budgets. Small wins here have massive impact.

4

Increase Income

While cutting expenses has limits, income growth is unlimited. Negotiate raises, switch jobs, build side income.

5

Tax Efficiency

Use ISAs and pensions to shelter investments from tax. Tax saved is money earned.

6

Stay the Course

Market dips are normal. Don't panic sell. Time in the market beats timing the market.

Common Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls on your journey to financial independence.

Lifestyle Inflation

As income grows, expenses often grow too. Keep lifestyle costs flat while income increases. Save your raises.

Timing the Market

Studies consistently show that time IN the market beats timing the market. Stick to your investment plan.

Ignoring Healthcare

Early retirees face healthcare costs before state pension age. Budget for private health insurance.

No Emergency Fund

Keep 3-6 months of expenses in liquid savings. This prevents selling investments at a loss during emergencies.

Neglecting the Present

FIRE is a marathon, not a sprint. Find balance between saving for tomorrow and living today.

No Plan for Retirement

Know what you'll do when you FIRE. Have hobbies, projects, or part-time work planned.
